  <Accordion title="How do I delete a chat?" icon="shapes">
    You can delete a chat by clicking the three dots (...) next to its name in the left-hand navigation panel on talk.shapes.inc. This will open a menu where you can choose to delete or rename the chat. Please note: DMs (a legacy chat type) are persistent and cannot be deleted. You can delete individual messages in a DM & choose to hide it from your sidebar if you wish.

  <Accordion title="How to delete my account?" icon="shapes">
    Open Shapes, Inc. If you are already in the app, stay there. If not, open [shapes.inc](https://shapes.inc) or [talk.shapes.inc](https://talk.shapes.inc) and sign in. Then open the sidebar menu and click **Account**. You can also go directly to `talk.shapes.inc/u/your-username/account`. Scroll to the bottom, find the **Delete account** card, and click **Delete account**.

    Shapes, Inc. will ask you to read a warning, click **Continue**, type your username, and then confirm. After that, a 30-second countdown starts. You can still click **Cancel** before the countdown finishes.

    - **What happens when you delete your account:**
      - **This action is permanent once the countdown finishes.**
      - Shapes and messages you created will remain unless you delete them first.
      - Chats you created will remain unless you delete them first.
      - Your account will be queued for permanent deletion and cannot be recovered.
      - You will not be able to sign up again with the same email.
      - The process may take up to 48 hours.
  </Accordion>

    **How Friends Work**

    - Follow someone by opening their profile from chat and clicking the heart icon.
    - If they follow you back, you become friends.
    - You can see followers and following from Search in the sidebar.

    Click their avatar in chat, then click the heart icon on their profile. Shapes, Inc. will tell you when you are following them.

  <Accordion title="Why did I get charged automatically for more credits?" icon="shapes">
    This occurs when you have auto top-up enabled. Auto top-up is enabled by default anytime you have a saved payment method. When you run out of account credits and have a payment method set up, your balance is automatically refilled once it runs low so your Shapes can keep running without interruption.

    Auto top-up is displayed during checkout when you purchase credits and explained in the Terms of Service  [here](http://shapes.inc/terms). This was also shared in our announcements when this feature was added in.

    If you prefer not to use auto top-up, you can disable auto top-up at any time by removing your saved payment method from your Payment History page. This can be found by going to [this page](https://shapes.inc/premium) and navigating to Payment History.

    Need help with a billing issue? Reach out to us anytime at [hi@shapes.inc](mailto:hi@shapes.inc)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why is a free engine costing credits?" icon="shapes">
    Free engines do not cost credits.

    Credits are only used when you trigger a response from a **premium engine**.

    If you’re seeing credits being used unexpectedly, it usually means a premium engine is being used instead of a free one. Please check the following settings:

    1. **Shape Dashboard → AI Engine**\
       Open your Shape’s dashboard and look at the **AI Engine** section in the user settings.
       - If you’re overriding the Shape’s default engine, make sure the selected engine is a **free engine**.
       - If you want to use the Shape’s primary engine, the override toggle should be **off**.
       - If using a primary AI Engine, the selected primary engine should be a free engine. This means that you won't see the warning that the engine costs credits below the selection.
    2. **AI Configurations → Prevent engine override**\
       Check whether **“Prevent engine override”** is enabled in your chat. You can find this settings menu by clicking on your chat title.
       - Turn this **on** if you do _not_ want your primary engine to be overridden.
       - Turn this **off** if you _do_ want to allow engine overrides.
    3. **Chat Settings → Personal AI Preferences**\
       Click the chat title to open chat settings and confirm that your **personal AI preference** is set to **“Don’t use my credits.”**
       - There is an **auto-upgrade** feature that will automatically switch your chat to a premium engine if credits are available.
       - Make sure this setting is enabled, then do a **hard refresh** and re-check the setting. 
       - This setting is user-specific, and each user will need to set this in a chat. The chat owner has no impact on this setting. 

    Some actions and premium engines may still require credits. Prices, available engines, and credit rules can change, so check the credit notice shown in chat before sending if you are trying to avoid credit usage.

    If you’re still having trouble, head to the [\*\*support \*\*](https://talk.shapes.inc/support)**chat** and we’ll help you troubleshoot.\
    Please be ready to invite a staff member to your chat and temporarily make them an **owner** so we can debug the issue for you.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why did I get invited to a chat?" icon="shapes">
    Shapes allows you to invite other users to join your chats at any time. To add someone, click on the **chat title** and choose one of the following options:

    - Share the **chat link** with the person you want to invite
    - Open the chat menu and select **“Invite human friends”**
    - Go to **Chat menu → Participants** and type a username to add them directly
    - (For eligible users) use features that allow inviting **all users** to a chat

    If you’d rather not be added to group chats with other people, you can disable this feature at any time.

    To enable a private account:\
    Go to the bottom of the left sidebar → click your **username** → **Settings** → toggle **“Private account”** on.

  <Accordion title="Why don’t the premium credit redemption options reset on the Rewards page?" icon="shapes">
    We design our free offerings to be generous **and** sustainable.

    Shapes provides free chat on many available engines, and the Rewards shop may include credit packs when they are available.

    Premium engines have real, ongoing provider costs. Because of this, free credit redemptions can be limited, may change over time, and may not reset like normal daily tasks.

    If you need more premium usage, you can:

    - Purchase additional credits directly on the Premium page
    - Complete Rewards tasks and redeem credit packs when they are available
    - Redeem or purchase Shapes gift cards

    This approach allows us to keep a large portion of Shapes free for everyone, while ensuring the platform remains available long-term.
