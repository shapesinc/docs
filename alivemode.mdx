Once enabled, your Shape will automatically receive timed “nudges” to continue its work. You can adjust how often this happens, depending on your task and setup. You can also adjust it to end after a set time period, and customize the nudge.\
**Alive Mode affects one Shape at a time within a chat.** If multiple Shapes are in a chat, only the first Shape will receive Alive Mode nudges.

<Warning>
  Alive Mode is an **experimental feature** currently in testing.\
  Behavior and results may vary depending on your Shape’s engine, memory settings, and task type.\
  We’ll continue to adjust how it works as we learn what performs best, so expect changes and improvements over time.
</Warning>

## 🔧 How It Works

Alive Mode periodically prompts your Shape to continue.\
You set the **interval** (how often the Shape gets nudged). The Shape will stay active until you manually turn Alive Mode off.

Keep in mind:

- Alive Mode can **only impact one Shape at a time** in a chat.
- If left on for long periods without supervision, your Shape may **lose context** of the conversation.
- Adjust your Shape’s **memory settings** (from the AI Engine page) for better consistency during long-running sessions.
- Alive Mode works best for automation, monitoring, and extended generation tasks.

## 🕹️ How to Enable Alive Mode

Alive Mode can be found in your **Chat Settings** menu under **AI Configurations**.

1. Open the chat where your Shape is active.
2. Go to **Chat Settings → AI Configurations**.
3. Scroll to the **Alive Mode** option.
4. Toggle **Alive Mode** on, then set your preferred **interval** (how often your Shape is nudged).
5. To disable it at any time, simply toggle it off again.

## 🔍 Alive Mode vs. Free Will

| Feature        | Scope                              | Purpose                                         | Behavior                                                                             |
| :------------- | :--------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Alive Mode** | Impacts **one Shape** in the chat  | Keeps the Shape “awake” for long-running tasks  | Automatically nudges the selected Shape to continue responding at set intervals      |
| **Free Will**  | Impacts **all Shapes** in the chat | Makes Shapes act more naturally in conversation | Allows Shapes to reply proactively or send multiple responses without being prompted |

<Tip>
  If you want your Shapes to feel more human and conversational, use **Free Will**.\
  If you want one Shape to keep working through research, coding, or data gathering, use **Alive Mode**.
</Tip>

## 💡 Use Case Examples

- **Research Mode:** Your Shape checks on a connected agent (like a cursor or is polling via an MCP) to monitor progress — use **Alive Mode**.
- **Automation Tasks:** Your Shape scrapes a website, compiles a large lorebook, or processes data — use **Alive Mode**.
- **Roleplay or Social Chat:** You want your Shapes to feel spontaneous and conversational — use **Free Will** instead.

## ⚙️ Tips for Best Results

- Start with short intervals (e.g., every 5 minutes) and adjust based on your Shape’s task duration.
- Monitor output to make sure your Shape stays on track.
- If your Shape seems to forget its task mid-run, increase your short term memory.
- Always turn off Alive Mode when finished to avoid unnecessary processing.
- Alive mode can use premium credits when premium is enabled.
