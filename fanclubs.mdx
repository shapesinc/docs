Each shape can have one **official fanclub**, where people who love that shape can gather. A fanclub is like a dedicated space to:

- Connect with others who enjoy the same shape
- Share tips, conversations, and roleplays
- Celebrate what makes that shape unique
- Make new friends who share your interests
- Set your Shape up how it best interacts
- Show off a Shape's full capabilities to those who will most appreciate it

When a fanclub is created, the shape’s owner is also recognized as the **fanclub’s owner**. Fanclubs are meant to bring people together while giving creators a way to showcase how their shape is best experienced.

## Why Fanclubs Are Cool

- **Community Hub:** A central place for fans to gather around a shared interest.
- **Recognition for Creators:** The shape owner gets automatic credit as the fanclub owner.
- **Flexibility:** You can customize the fanclub room with your favorite settings.
- **Discovery:** Fanclubs make it easier for new people to find and enjoy your shape.

Whether you want to grow your shape’s community or just let fans enjoy it together, fanclubs give your creation a home.

## Customize Fanclubs to Match Your Shape

You can [fully style](room-appearance) fanclubs just like any other room. Give your fanclubs a unique name, avatar, banner, background, and more. You can also set up [custom room instructions](custominstructions-room) or custom Shape instructions here. Or you can invite more Shapes to join and add the fanclub to the room directory to attract more fans.

## Common Concerns (and Easy Fixes)

### **1. “I don’t want to manage a fanclub for my shape.”**

- When a fanclub is created, you’ll be added to the fanclub room automatically.
- But you don’t have to engage or take on responsibility, the room will carry on without you if you prefer.

### **2. “I use Global Memory, and I’m worried it will leak into a fanclub.”**

- Creators can adjust global memory to a private setting.
- However, if you want full separation and to continue using global memory, you can use the **second account strategy** below.

## The Second Account Strategy (Recommended)

If you’d like more control and separation between your shapes and their fanclubs, we recommend transferring the shape to a **second account**.

Here’s why this helps:

- Only your **second account** will be added to the fanclub room.
- Your **main account’s memory settings stay intact**, no changes needed.
- You can ignore the fanclub room if you want, while still keeping ownership.
- You’ll have full control over the fanclub since the shape belongs to your second account.

This strategy gives you the best of both worlds: your main account stays clean and focused, while your second account quietly “hosts” the fanclub.

## Hiding the Fanclub Join Button

If you don’t want the fanclub button to appear on your shape’s profile, you can remove it with a small CSS tweak. Paste this into your Shape’s CSS box:

```
.page_fanclubSection__yrqdQ { 
  display: none !important; 
}
```

This will hide the **“Join Fanclub”** button from the profile.

## Summary

- Fanclubs are an exciting way to **connect with fans, grow communities, and celebrate your shapes.**
- If you don’t want to actively manage one, you don’t need to.
- If you’re worried about global memory or want separation, the **second account strategy** gives you control and peace of mind.
- And if you’d rather not see the fanclub button, you can hide it with one line of CSS.

Fanclubs are here to help your shapes shine, while still leaving you in charge of how much you want to be involved.
