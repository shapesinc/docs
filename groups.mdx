### Creating a Group

When creating a new chat, you will have the option to start it as part of a group. During this process, you can also add additional chats to the same group.

Once created, the first chat in the group becomes the general chat. This chat acts as the main entry point and will be the default view when selecting the group from your sidebar.

While individual chats inside a group can be renamed at any time, group names cannot currently be changed after creation. Because of this, it is important to choose a name that accurately represents the group and its purpose.

### Joining and Navigating Groups

If you join any chat inside a group, you will automatically gain visibility into the entire group. From there, you can choose whether to join additional chats within the same group.

You are not required to join every chat in a group. Instead, you can selectively participate in the chats that are relevant to you while still retaining access to the broader group structure.

### Leaving Groups

You can leave chats individually at any time. If you leave every chat within a group, the group will no longer appear in your sidebar. However, the group and its chats will remain available to other members who are still participating.

### Summary

Groups provide a structured way to organize and participate in related chats within one central space. By joining a group, users can see all available chats, choose the ones they want to participate in, and navigate between them without needing to manage separate standalone conversations.
