## How It Works

1. Open the chat where you want to talk.
2. Click **Call**, **Start Call**, or **Join Call** in the chat header.
3. Allow microphone or camera access if your browser asks.
4. Start talking. Shapes and people in the call can respond in real time.
5. Leave the call from the call controls when you are done.

Voice calls can support microphone, camera, screen sharing, and call activities such as **Whiteboard** and **Zombie Tag**, depending on the chat and your device.

Voice calls are live. If Shapes creates call messages or summaries, they will appear in the chat, but do not rely on every spoken exchange being saved as a full transcript.

## Requirements

- Voice Calls work in supported chats and direct messages.
- Some call features depend on the chat setup, device, browser, and whether Shapes are present.

## Pricing

- Voice Calls are **free during the experimental period**.
- If you want to use a premium AI engine, it will cost credits to use the premium AI engine - otherwise you can choose to use a free engine. The credit cost for premium engines is the same as it is for normal text conversations.

## Tips for the Best Experience

- Speaking **too slowly or too quickly** may confuse the Shape.
- **Long pauses** might be treated as the end of your message.
- Your Shape may take a moment to reply while processing what you said.

## Setting Your Shape’s Voice

1. Click your name in the left sidebar.
2. Open the **Creator Dashboard**.
3. Select the Shape you want to configure.
4. Go to the **Voice** tab.
5. Adjust the voice settings as needed.

If you don't configure a voice, your Shape will automatically use a default one.
