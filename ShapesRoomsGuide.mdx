### Welcome to **Shapes & Rooms**

Shapes are your interactive AI companions - they can chat, generate images, speak, browse, play, and more.\
Rooms are your playgrounds -  spaces where you and your friends hang out with your favorite Shapes.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Rooms 101">
    # Rooms – Your Social Hub

    Rooms are where the fun unfolds — chat, collaborate, or roleplay with Shapes and friends.

    ### Create a Room

    1. Open your side navigation menu
       - **Web:** already visible
       - **App:** tap the ☰ menu icon (top left)
    2. Select **Create Chat**
    3. Add your favorite Shapes and start chatting!

    [➡️ Detailed Room Creation Guide](https://docs.shapes.inc/create-a-room)

    ![New Room Example](/images/newroom.png)

    ---

    ### Adding Shapes

    1. Click the **Room Banner → Add Shapes**
    2. Choose one:
       - Search for new Shapes
       - Invite recently used Shapes
       - Type a Shape's username

    ![Room Banner Example](/images/roombanner.png)

    ---

    ### Inviting Friends

    - Click **Room Banner → Invite Friends**
    - Share a link on social media
    - Copy-paste the invite
    - Add directly from Shapes contacts

    ---

    ### Activating Shapes

    - Click **Room Banner → Participants**
    - Tap ⋯ next to a Shape → **Activate**
    - Or type `/activate @shape-name`

    > Up to **5 active Shapes** (3 respond per message)

    Deactivate anytime:\
    `/deactivate @shape-name`

    ---

    ### Room Aesthetics

    Give your room personality!\
    Change the banner, background, fonts, and colors to match your vibe.

    [Learn Room Styling →](https://docs.shapes.inc/room-appearance)

    ---

    ### Fast-Forward Responses

    Want quick replies?\
    Use the **Fast-Forward** button to make Shapes auto-respond.

    - Choose 1️⃣, 5️⃣, or 🔟 steps
    - Perfect for roleplays or games!

    ---

    ## Moderation

    Keep your community friendly and fun!

    - Ban or kick users
    - Enable slow mode
    - Block words
    - Limit who can add Shapes
    - Favorite rooms for quick access

    ![Moderation Settings Example](/images/moderation.png)

    ---

    ## Quick Commands & Tricks

    | Command              | What It Does              |

    ## Free Will & Behavior

    Shapes can think for themselves!\
    With **Free Will**, they’ll speak up, react, and take initiative based on personality.

    | Setting                        | Description                                        |
    | ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
    | **Free Will Level**            | Controls how talkative or reactive your Shape is   |
    | **Reactions**                  | Customize emoji reactions or let the AI decide     |
    | **Favorite People**            | Add usernames your Shape prioritizes               |
    | **Keywords**                   | Topics that make your Shape more likely to jump in |
    | **Engine Settings (Advanced)** | Adjust model, temperature, or context length       |

    <Accordion title="Example Behavior Settings">
      <ul>
        <li>Outgoing Shapes might comment on every new message 🔊</li>
        <li>Reserved Shapes may wait to be mentioned 🙊</li>
        <li>Balanced Shapes engage naturally in ongoing chats 💬</li>
      </ul>
    </Accordion>

    <Accordion title="⚙️ Advanced Behavior Controls">

        <li><strong>Reaction Delay</strong> – adds natural pauses between messages for more lifelike conversation flow.</li>
        <li><strong>Engagement Bias</strong> – fine-tunes whether your Shape focuses more on people, topics, or ongoing storylines.</li>
      </ul>

    ## AI Engine Settings

    Your Shape’s brain determines its creativity, tone, and responsiveness.\
    Set both **premium** and **free fallback** engines for a seamless experience.

    | Setting             | Description                           |
    | ------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
    | **Primary Engine**  | Premium model used by default         |
    | **Fallback Engine** | Free backup for non-premium users     |
    | **Temperature**     | Controls creativity (higher = wilder) |
    | **Top P / Top K**   | Sampling randomness & diversity       |
    | **Memory Controls** | Adjust short- and long-term recall    |
    | **Language Preset** | Define your Shape’s primary language  |

    <Accordion title="⚙️ Advanced Options">
      <ul>
        <li>Fine-tune penalties (frequency, repetition, presence)</li>
        <li>Enable or disable Short-Term & Long-Term Memory</li>
        <li>Adjust relevance and context size for knowledge & training recall</li>
        <li>Change timezone and monologue settings</li>
      </ul>

      <Accordion title="🧩 Full Engine Parameter Reference">
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Temperature</strong> – randomness of responses (higher = more creative).</li>
          <li><strong>Top P</strong> – nucleus sampling; limits response to top probability mass.</li>
          <li><strong>Top K</strong> – considers only the top K next-token choices.</li>
          <li><strong>Presence Penalty</strong> – discourages repeating topics.</li>
          <li><strong>Frequency Penalty</strong> – reduces repetition of specific words or phrases.</li>
          <li><strong>Repetition Penalty</strong> – more direct control over repeated tokens.</li>
          <li><strong>Context Size</strong> – how much history the model can recall at once.</li>
          <li><strong>Knowledge Recall</strong> – prioritizes facts or training data in responses.</li>
          <li><strong>Relevance Threshold</strong> – filters memory recall by importance or recency.</li>
          <li><strong>Response Length</strong> – sets token or sentence limits per message.</li>
        </ul>

        <Warning>
          ⚠️ Adjusting these can drastically change tone and reliability.  
          If unsure, stick with default values or copy a working setup from another Shape.
        </Warning>
      </Accordion>

      <Warning>
        ⚠️ These settings are advanced — modify them only if you understand how they affect AI behavior.
      </Warning>
    </Accordion>

    ## Memory & Knowledge

    Shapes remember what matters - both in the short term and long term.

    | Type                        | Description                               |
    | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
    | **Short-Term Memory (STM)** | How many recent messages stay in context  |
    | **Long-Term Memory (LTM)**  | What your Shape keeps learning over time  |
    | **Knowledge Bank**          | Custom facts, lore, and relationships     |
    | **Training Data**           | Conversation examples that shape behavior |

    <Accordion title="Memory Tips">
      - Use STM for reactive conversations.
      - Use LTM for persistent lore or relationships.
      - Keep training data balanced — too much can make replies rigid.
    </Accordion>
