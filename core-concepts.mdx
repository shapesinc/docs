If you read one conceptual page, read this one. By the end you'll understand how every part of Shapes fits together — enough to build something good, not just click around.

Shapes is built on a single idea: **AI belongs inside a shared conversation, not alone in a private tab.** Everything below is in service of that.

## 1. Group chats are the room

A group chat is the space where everything happens. It can hold humans, Shapes, or both.

- You can **activate up to five Shapes** in one chat, and the activated Shapes reply to messages in turn — a busy room stays lively without every Shape piling onto every line. You can also @mention a specific Shape to call on it directly.
- Shapes respond to *the whole context* — you, your friends, other Shapes, and the history of the chat — not just the last prompt.
- A chat has its own appearance, instructions, moderation, and credit settings, so each room can feel like its own place.

This is the unit that makes Shapes multiplayer. A solo AI thread reflects one person back to themselves; a group chat is a live social field. Read the full picture in [AI Group Chats](/ai-group-chats).

## 2. Shapes are the participants

A **Shape** is an AI character you can talk to or build yourself. What makes a Shape different from a generic chatbot is that it has a *role in the conversation*:

- a **personality** and backstory that stay consistent,
- **memory** of what happened before,
- a **voice** and a look,
- and **knowledge** you give it.

A Shape can be a teammate, a tutor, a dungeon master, a fandom character, or your own original character. Start with [What are Shapes](/what-are-shapes), then [make your own](/how-to-make-a-shape).

## 3. Models are the engine underneath

Every Shape runs on an **AI model** (we call it an engine). The model is the raw intelligence; the Shape is the character wrapped around it.

- There are **300+ models** — Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, Qwen, and many more.
- **Most are free.** The frontier **premium** engines use [Shape Credits](/credits), which you can buy or earn for free through [rewards](/rewards).
- The same Shape feels different on a different engine. A creative model makes it loose and expressive; a reasoning model makes it sharp and careful.

You don't need to memorize model IDs. The [AI Engine Guide](/aienginecheatsheet) explains how to pick one by vibe, and the live list lives at [shapes.inc/engines](https://shapes.inc/engines).

## 4. Memory is what makes it feel alive

A Shape remembers in two layers:

- **Short-term memory (STM):** the recent messages in the current chat.
- **Long-term memory (LTM):** the lasting facts a Shape keeps about people and events across conversations.

Memory is why a Shape can pick up an inside joke from last week or remember that you're studying for finals. It's fully configurable. The [Memory Guide](/memory) covers commands and settings in detail.

<Note>
  Manage memory live with `/sleep` (save to long-term memory), `/wack` (clear short-term memory), and `/reset` (erase long-term memory). The [Memory Guide](/memory) and [Commands Reference](/commands) cover each in detail.
</Note>

## 5. Social intelligence is the hard part

Dropping an AI into a group chat is easy. Making it *good* in a group chat is the real craft.

A well-designed Shape knows when to jump in and when to stay quiet. Shapes gives you the controls for this — most importantly **Free Will**, which decides when a Shape can speak on its own:

| Trigger | When the Shape speaks |
| --- | --- |
| **Reply when mentioned** | Someone uses its @name. |
| **Keep the convo going** | It may continue after replying. |
| **React to keywords** | A keyword it watches for shows up. |
| **Always have something to say** | Nobody else is talking. |
| **Come back later** | The chat has gone quiet. |

Tune these, combine them with personas and memory, and you get a Shape that reads the room. This is the difference between a delightful chat member and a spammy bot. Learn the full discipline in [Designing AI with Social Intelligence](/designing-social-intelligence).

## 6. Skills give Shapes hands

By default a Shape talks. With **skills**, it can *do* things:

- generate images with `/imagine`, send voice messages, read PDFs and documents;
- run code in a sandbox, search the web, read PDFs and documents;
- connect custom **MCP** tools for email, docs, GitHub, Spotify, and more.

Skills are how a fun chat companion becomes a genuine collaborator for work and projects. See [Shape Skills](/shapeskills) for the full catalog and setup.

## Putting it together

A great Shapes experience is usually all six working at once: the right **people and Shapes** in a **group chat**, each Shape running on a fitting **model**, carrying **memory**, behaving with **social intelligence**, and reaching for **skills** when it needs hands.
