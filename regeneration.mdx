## **What Happens Each Regeneration**

Every regeneration runs the full pipeline again:

1. **Inputs gathered**
   - Your Shape’s personality inputs
   - Relevant knowledge (based on your Shape’s knowledge settings)
   - Short- and long-term memories (based on your Shape’s memory settings)
   - Training (based on your Shape’s training information)
2. **Prompt built**\
   All of the above is combined into a single input for the model.
3. **Fresh generation**\
   The AI Engine calculates a new answer token by token, using randomness and probabilities.

   <Tip>
     Premium user tip: This is why regenerations cost the same as a new message, it’s the same full process each time.
   </Tip>

## **Why Responses Differ**

Each run is like rolling a weighted set of dice. The outcome can vary depending on:

- **Randomness settings**: Higher temperature/top-p means more creative and unexpected responses; lower settings mean more focused, consistent ones.
- **Prompt wording**: The way you phrase your request guides the model toward different directions.
- **Context richness**: Adding more detail in your instructions or conversation opens up more possible paths.
- **Chance**: Even with the same setup, probability can land on a different “branch” of the answer space.

## **Tips for Maximizing Variety**

If you want regenerations that explore different angles, try:

- Raising **temperature/top-p** to encourage creativity.
- Rephrasing your question to push for new perspectives.
- Providing more context or constraints to steer variety.
- Changing your AI Engine or Shape prompting if the responses aren't what you're looking for.

  <Warning>
    Use caution when adjusting parameters like temperature and top p: these make a huge impact\! 
  </Warning>

## **Key Takeaway**

Regeneration isn’t about tweaking an answer, it’s about giving the AI a fresh roll of the dice with the same setup. Sometimes you’ll get a similar answer, sometimes something totally new, and often something in between.

Think of it as exploring multiple “what ifs” with the same starting conditions.
