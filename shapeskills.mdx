Skills give your Shape hands — the ability to *do* things, not just talk about them. Connect the apps you already use (Notion, Gmail, Spotify, Google Drive, GitHub) and your Shape stops being a chat window and starts being a collaborator.

<Note>
  This page is the **inspiration**. For the complete, verified list of what a Shape can do and exactly how to switch each capability on, see the [Capabilities catalog](/capabilities).
</Note>

## It's genuinely magical

The best part is watching a Shape reach for a tool on its own — pulling up a song that fits the mood, saving a note mid-roleplay, or fetching the morning's news because that's just what it does. With the right skills, a Shape can:

- Build and update a **Lorebook in Notion** it references throughout a roleplay.
- **Triage your inbox** and draft replies in Gmail.
- Add a song to a **Spotify** playlist based on the vibe of the chat.
- Research the web and assemble a **Google Slides** deck from what it learns.
- Spin up a quick **webpage**, a meme, or a soundtrack for your story.

## Add a custom skill in two minutes

Beyond the built-ins, you can give a Shape brand-new tools through **MCP** providers like [Smithery](https://smithery.ai). Here's the flow, using a meme generator as an example:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open AI Configurations">
    In a chat you own, open **Chat Settings → AI Configurations**, then scroll to **Custom Skills** and toggle it on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grab an endpoint URL">
    On [smithery.ai](https://smithery.ai), find a tool (search "meme"), connect any account it needs, and choose **"Get URL with keys"** to copy your endpoint URL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste it into Shapes">
    Back in **Custom Skills**, paste the URL and give the skill a clear name (e.g. *Imgflip Memes*).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Just ask">
    Your Shape now has the tool. Talk to it naturally — "make me a meme about Mondays" — and it'll use the skill, mixing in its own personality.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Which engines can use skills?

Tool use depends on the model. Look for the **Tools** badge on the [engines page](https://shapes.inc/engines), and use [Which Model Should I Use?](/choosing-a-model) to pick one that's good at it. If a skill isn't firing, try switching to a Tools-capable engine first.

## Tips for great results

- **Name skills clearly** so you remember what each one does.
- **Try different engines** — not every model uses tools the same way.
- **Mix skills with personality** — a roleplay Shape that quietly keeps a Notion Lorebook feels alive in a way a plain bot never will.
- Skills are **experimental** — some third-party integrations may be flaky. If one misbehaves, check it's enabled and your engine supports tools.
