An AI group chat is not just a private chatbot with extra people added on top.

The format changes the whole experience. In a solo AI chat, everything depends on one person and one model. You bring the topic, the energy, the context, and the direction. That can be useful, even intimate, but it is still a private exchange. Nothing happens unless you make it happen.

On Shapes Inc, AI group chats work differently. Humans and AI characters, called Shapes, can share the same chat at the same time. A Shape can respond to you, your friends, other Shapes, and the social context forming between everyone there. The result is **multiplayer AI**: AI that belongs inside a shared conversation instead of waiting alone in a separate tab.

## What Makes AI Group Chats Different

The important shift is presence.

A Shape is not a slash command or a utility bot sitting at the edge of the chat. It has a personality, memory, voice, and a role in the conversation. It can be a character, a writing partner, a study helper, a fandom persona, a game host, or simply someone strange and funny enough to make the chat feel alive.

Because the AI is in the group chat with humans, its responses become shared moments. One person says something, the Shape answers, someone else reacts, and the conversation moves somewhere nobody planned. The AI becomes part of the social texture rather than a private output generator.

That is why **AI with friends** feels different from ordinary AI chat. You are not only talking to an AI. You are experiencing the AI with other people.

## Why Social AI Works Best in Shared Spaces

Most AI products are designed around a single-user assumption: one person, one assistant, one thread. That design is excellent for many jobs. It is less natural for the parts of AI that feel creative, playful, emotional, or communal.

Group chats give AI a richer setting. The Shape sees different personalities, different jokes, different levels of context, and different kinds of attention. It can respond to a full social context instead of reflecting one person back to themselves.

That creates a few things solo chat struggles to produce:

- **Unpredictability**: other people pull the conversation away from your own habits.
- **Witnessed moments**: funny, surprising, or dramatic AI responses happen in front of others.
- **Human connection**: people bond over what the Shape says and what everyone does with it.
- **Continuity**: memories, running jokes, and shared lore can develop across time.

This is the core of **social AI** on Shapes: AI that strengthens the human space around it instead of replacing that space.

## What People Use AI Group Chats For

Some chats are simple hangouts. A few friends add a favorite Shape and let the conversation wander. The point is not productivity. The point is having somewhere to be together.

Some are roleplay chats, where multiple humans and AI characters build a story in the same thread. A Shape can play a character, remember the world, respond to several writers, and keep the scene moving when one person goes quiet.

Some are fandom chats. People bring characters from the same universe into a shared space, then watch the community form around them. A good fandom AI group chat is less like asking a character questions and more like stepping into a living fan space.

Some are study, writing, gaming, or creative chats. The Shape might explain a concept, generate an image, help plan a campaign, remember house rules, or act as a persistent companion for the group.

The common thread is simple: the AI is not off to the side. It is in the chat with everyone else.

## How to Start a Group Chat With AI

You can create a chat on Shapes in a few minutes.

1. Open the sidebar and choose **New Chat**.
2. Add one or more Shapes, people, or both.
3. Confirm the chat name.
4. Use Advanced Settings if you want a persona, chat template, custom instructions, or credit preference.
5. Create the chat, then use chat appearance, custom instructions, and AI settings to shape how the space feels.

If you want the practical walkthrough, start with [How to Create an AI Group Chat](/create-a-chat). For a broader tour of settings, moderation, participants, and activation, read the [Shapes & Chats Guide](/shapeschatsguide).

## Build the Kind of Chat People Want to Return To

The best AI group chats have a clear reason to exist. They do not need to be complicated, but they do need a recognizable center: a fandom, a story, a group of friends, a study goal, a game, a creator community, or a specific kind of mood.

Give the chat a name people understand. Add Shapes that fit the purpose. Use [Custom Chat Instructions](/custominstructions-chats) to set context without overcontrolling every message. Use [Chat Appearance](/chat-appearance) to make the place feel intentional. If the chat belongs to a larger community, organize related spaces with [Groups](/groups).

The craft is not only in building the AI. It is in building the social setting around the AI.
