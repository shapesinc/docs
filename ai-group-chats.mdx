A group chat on Shapes can hold people, AI characters (we call them [Shapes](/what-are-shapes)), or both at once. That's the whole idea: you experience the AI *with other people*.

Picture your group chat with friends. Now add an AI character to it. It reads the conversation, jumps in when it makes sense, cracks jokes, helps out, and remembers what happened last time. Everyone sees it happen together. That's a multiplayer AI group chat, and right now Shapes is the only place you can have one.

## Why it feels different

When you chat with AI alone, you bring everything: the topic, the energy, the next question. It's useful, but quiet.

Put that same AI in a chat with friends and it comes alive. One person says something, the Shape replies, someone else reacts, and the conversation goes somewhere none of you planned. The funny moments happen in front of everyone. People bond over what the Shape says. Running jokes and shared memories build up over time.

## What people use them for

- **Hangouts.** A few friends and a favorite Shape, just talking.
- **Roleplay and stories.** Several writers and AI characters build a scene together, and the Shape keeps track of the world.
- **Fandoms.** Bring characters from a show or game into a shared space and watch a community form around them.
- **Getting stuff done.** Studying, planning, gaming, or working, with a Shape that helps and remembers the details.

## How to start one

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open New Chat">
    In the sidebar, choose **New Chat**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Shapes, people, or both">
    Search for a Shape, pick people to invite, or both.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name it and create">
    Confirm the chat name and you're in. Then invite more friends with the chat link.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can activate up to **five Shapes** in one chat. When you post, the activated Shapes reply in turn. Want just one to answer? `@mention` it. To learn how a Shape decides when to speak, read [Designing Social Intelligence](/designing-social-intelligence).

For the full setup walkthrough, see [Create an AI Group Chat](/create-a-chat). For the in-chat controls, see [Chats 101](/chats-101).
