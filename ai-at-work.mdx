Most AI at work is solo: everyone opens their own private tab, asks their own questions, and the answers never leave that tab. Your team never sees the reasoning, the context, or the work.

Shapes flips that. A Shapes group chat is a room your whole team is in — and the AI is in it too. Think of it like an **AI Slack**: the same channels and threads you already understand, except an AI teammate is present, reads the conversation, and pitches in when it's useful. The collaboration is shared, not siloed.

<Info>
  Shapes is a chat app, not a Slack plugin. The "AI Slack" here is the Shapes group chat itself — a room with your teammates and an AI in it. To connect outside tools like email, docs, GitHub, or Linear, you use [Shape Skills](/shapeskills), covered below.
</Info>

## Why a shared room beats a private tab

- **Context is visible.** When the AI answers in the channel, everyone sees the question and the answer. No re-explaining, no copy-paste.
- **The AI has memory of the team, not just you.** It can remember decisions, naming conventions, and who owns what — see [Memory](/memory).
- **Many minds, one thread.** A teammate asks the follow-up you wouldn't have. The AI adjusts to the group, not to one person's assumptions.
- **It never gets tired of the boring parts.** Summaries, changelogs, and "what did we decide?" are exactly what an always-present Shape is good at.

## Set up a team chat

1. [Create a group chat](/create-a-chat) for your team or project.
2. Invite teammates with the chat link, and add one or more [Shapes](/what-are-shapes) for the roles you need — a coordinator, a writer, a researcher.
3. Give the room shared context with [Custom Chat Instructions](/custominstructions-chats): who's on the team, what you're building, the tone you want.
4. Pin the essentials — links, rules, current goals — so newcomers catch up instantly.
5. Decide how forward the AI should be using [Free Will](/designing-social-intelligence). For a work room, "reply when mentioned" plus "keep the convo going" is usually the right balance — present, not noisy.

## Patterns that work

- **Standups in a channel.** Ask the Shape to summarize what changed since yesterday and what's blocked. Everyone reads the same recap.
- **"What did we decide?"** Long thread, lots of opinions — ask the Shape to summarize the decision and the open questions.
- **Spin off a deep-dive.** Use [Branch Off](/branch-off) on a message to pull a side discussion into its own chat (it carries the recent context with it) without derailing the main room.
- **Specialist Shapes.** Give each Shape a job — one writes, one researches, one keeps notes — and [activate](/chats-101) the ones you need. Up to five can be active and they reply in turn, so a small "team of agents" stays coherent.

## Keep the room sane

Work chats need guardrails more than fun ones do:

- Use [moderation](/chats-101) to set slow mode, limit who can add Shapes, and block words.
- Keep Free Will conservative so Shapes don't talk over people in focused discussions.
- Put standing context in [Custom Chat Instructions](/custominstructions-chats) instead of repeating it every message.
