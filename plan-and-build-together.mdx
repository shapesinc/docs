Solo AI is great for solo work. But a lot of the things you actually want to get done involve other people: planning a trip with friends, studying for the same exam, sketching out an app idea with a co-founder.

Shapes is built for exactly that. Put the people and an AI in one [group chat](/ai-group-chats) and the work becomes shared — everyone sees the same plan, pushes on the same draft, and remembers the same decisions.

## Plan a trip with friends

Group trips die in scattered DMs. Pull everyone into one chat with a Shape that helps organize it.

- Brainstorm destinations and dates together; the Shape can [search the web](/shapeskills) for ideas, weather, and prices.
- Let it keep the running itinerary in [memory](/memory) so "what did we decide on day 2?" always has an answer.
- Pin the plan so latecomers catch up instantly.
- When one thread gets deep — say, just the food plan — [branch it off](/branch-off) into its own chat without losing the group.

## Study together

A study chat is one of the most natural fits for multiplayer AI: an AI tutor explains something, a friend challenges it, and the Shape adjusts to where the *group* is confused.

- Add a tutor Shape on a strong [reasoning engine](/aienginecheatsheet) and give it your syllabus as [knowledge](/shape-settings#knowledge).
- Use [Skip Turn and Fast Forward](/multimessages) to have a Shape keep explaining or quiz the group without waiting for a prompt each time.
- With [skills](/shapeskills), a Shape can pull papers, do math, translate, and crawl source material so everyone studies from the same brief.
- Memory means the Shape remembers what your group already covered and what tripped you up.

## What makes the group version better

- **Shared memory.** The plan, the syllabus, the spec — the Shape holds the thread so no one person has to.
- **Faster iteration.** Someone reacts, someone else builds on it, the Shape revises. Ideas move through the whole group's energy.
- **One source of truth.** Instead of five private AI chats with five different answers, there's one room everyone trusts.

## Set it up

1. [Create a chat](/create-a-chat) for the project and invite the people involved.
2. Add a Shape suited to the job and run it on a fitting [model](/aienginecheatsheet).
3. Give the room [custom instructions](/custominstructions-chats) and feed the Shape any [knowledge](/shape-settings#knowledge) it needs.
4. Connect [skills](/shapeskills) if you want it to research, generate, or build.
5. Tune [Free Will](/designing-social-intelligence) so the Shape is helpful without hijacking the conversation.
