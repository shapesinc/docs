## Why Group Chats Die

Group chats usually fail in one of two ways.

They're too quiet: functional, used for logistics, but never really alive. People drop in, say what needs saying, and leave. Nobody's actually *there*.

Or they're too noisy: the popular ones become overwhelming fast. Conversations scroll past before anyone can react. The intimacy that made it good in the first place disappears under volume.

The core problem in both cases is the same: **a group chat is only as alive as its most active participants** — and people have limited energy. Real life intervenes. Time zones diverge. Conversations stall.

What if there were a participant who was always present, always engaged, and never needed a break?
