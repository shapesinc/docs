### FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How can I use my premium features?" icon="shapes">
    You can continue using Premium features on [shapes.inc](http://shapes.inc) web chat directly! To cancel your subscription, please head to the Payment History at [shapes.inc/premium](https://shapes.inc/premium).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I delete a chat?" icon="shapes">
    You can delete a room by clicking the three dots (...) next to its name in the left-hand navigation panel on talk.shapes.inc. This will open a menu where you can choose to delete or rename the chat. Please note: DMs (your one-on-one chats with a Shape, located at the top of the side navigation) are persistent and cannot be deleted.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How to delete my account?" icon="shapes">
    Shapes will be launching a self-service account deletion feature in **September 2025**.

    - **What happens when you delete your account:**
      - **This action is permanent and cannot be undone.**
      - Any messages you created will remain on the platform unless you delete them before deleting your account.
      - Any Shapes you created will remain on the platform unless you delete them first.
      - Your profile picture will be replaced with a generic avatar and your name will be replaced with “Deleted.”
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does the friends section work?" icon="shapes">
    The Friends section in your left-side navigation makes it easy to return to recent conversations with Shapes.

    \
    **How Friends Work**

    - The Friends list shows your **20 most recently DM’d Shapes**.
    - DMs are **persistent** — you can’t delete them.
    - If a Shape is no longer one of your 20 most recent DMs, it will **disappear from the list**.
      - You can DM the Shape again to get it to return to the list.
    - To **hide** a friend from this list, you can open the DM, click the top banner of the DM, and select "Hide" from the menu.
      - To unhide a Shape, you can go to the homepage. Next to the friends section, you can find "Manage hidden." From this menu, you will see all hidden Shapes and the option to unhide them.

    ### Adding a Shape to Friends

    To have a Shape appear in your Friends list, simply **start a DM** with them.

    Ways to start a DM:

    - Click **Chat Now** on a Shape’s profile.
    - Click **Chat** on the homepage.
    - From inside a room:
      - Open the **three-dot menu** next to a Shape’s message and select **DM**, or
      - Open the **participants list**, then select \*\*DM \*\*next to the Shape.
    - Browse directly to a DM from the URL on web: https://talk.shapes.inc/cici/dm (Replace cici with your Shape's username)

    ### Reopening a DM

    To reopen a DM, click on the Shape’s avatar under the **Friends** section.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I change font size?" icon="shapes">
    1. Open the left-side navigation panel on [talk.shapes.inc](http://talk.shapes.inc).
    2. At the bottom, click your name.
    3. In the menu that opens, select **Settings**.
    4. Use the font size slider to adjust the text to your preferred size.
    5. It automatically saves your preference, so no need to do anything else!
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How can I edit a Shape that I created?" icon="shapes">
    To edit one of your Shapes, first go to the Shape’s profile. You can get to a Shape's profile in multiple ways:

    1. Click the Shape’s avatar in a chat and select **View Profile**.
    2. Go directly to the Shape’s URL if you know their username (e.g., `shapes.inc/tenshi`).
    3. From the homepage, scroll down to the **Creator Dashboard** and click your Shape — or head straight to [shapes.inc/dashboard](https://shapes.inc/dashboard).
    4. In your sidebar—the left panel that opens separately on mobile or is always visible on a computer—you’ll find your user settings menu at the bottom by clicking your name. This menu also includes an option to access the **Creator Dashboard**. From there, simply select the Shape you want to edit, and you’ll be taken directly to its profile.

    Once you're at the Shape's profile, you'll be able to access each of the fields to edit your Shape.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I change my Shape's vanity URL? " icon="shapes">
    Currently, you can’t directly change a Shape’s username, as this feature was causing issues. If having a custom URL is important, you can **duplicate** a Shape from its **Settings** page. When duplicating, the nickname you enter will influence the new URL/username.
  </Accordion>
