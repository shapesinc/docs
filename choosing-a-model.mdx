Every Shape runs on an AI **model** (we call it an engine), and the same character feels different depending on which one. There are **300+** to choose from and most are free. You don't need to memorize model IDs — pick by what you're doing, test it in chat, and swap if it feels off.

<Tip>
  This is the fast "what do I pick?" guide. For the family-by-family vibes (Claude vs GPT vs Gemini vs DeepSeek and friends) and what each badge means, read the [AI Engine Guide](/aienginecheatsheet). The live, always-current list is at [shapes.inc/engines](https://shapes.inc/engines).
</Tip>

## Pick by what you're doing

| If you want… | Reach for | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Writing & roleplay** | Claude, GLM, DeepSeek, MiniMax, or a roleplay-tuned engine | Expressive, stays in character, good at long scenes. |
| **A smart all-around helper** | Claude Sonnet, Gemini, Kimi, or GPT | Reliable, follows instructions, handles mixed tasks. |
| **Coding & reasoning** | GPT, Claude, DeepSeek, Qwen, Gemini Pro, or GLM | Careful, structured, good at hard problems. |
| **Free daily chat** | Gemma, Gemini Flash/Lite, GPT Nano/Mini, Llama, Mistral, or Qwen | Fast, capable, and free. |
| **Creative, less filtered chat** | Gemini, Grok, or DeepSeek | Looser, more surprising replies. |
| **Understanding images** | Anything with the **Native Vision** badge | Can read images people send. |

These are starting points, not rules. The best engine for a Shape is the one that feels right when you actually chat with it.

## Read the badges

Each engine on the directory carries badges that tell you what it's good at:

- **Reasoning** — stronger at careful thinking, planning, coding, and hard questions.
- **Native Vision** — can understand images.
- **Tools** — can use [Shape Skills](/shapeskills) like web search and code.
- **Free** — costs no credits.
- **Premium** — uses Shape Credits.
- **New** — recently added.
- **Unstable** — may fail or behave unpredictably. Fine for experiments; don't make it a Shape's main engine.

## Set a primary and a fallback

In a Shape's [AI Engine settings](/shape-settings#ai-engine) you choose two:

- **Primary** — the engine that powers your Shape day to day.
- **Fallback** — what it switches to if the primary is momentarily unavailable, so conversations don't stall.

Pick a strong primary for the vibe you want, and a dependable free engine as the fallback.

## How to actually decide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with a default">
    Use a well-rounded free engine. It's good enough to get your Shape talking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Match it to the job">
    Use the table above. Roleplay Shape? Try an expressive model. Coding mentor? Try a reasoning model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test in a real chat">
    Send a handful of messages. Does it sound right? Too long, too flat, too filtered? [Regenerate](/regeneration) a few replies to feel its range.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Swap freely">
    Changing engines is one click and doesn't reset your Shape. Try two or three before you settle.
  </Step>
</Steps>
