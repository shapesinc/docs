## What's Included in the Context Window?

Shapes combines several key inputs into its context window:

**User Input in Personality Fields** – Your Shape's name, backstory, and personality settings are included to maintain consistent tone and character throughout the conversation.

**AI Engine Preset** – Each AI engine preset establishes its fundamental style and behavioral patterns. This is where you write how you want your Shape to respond to you. Some options include, "Short Roleplay," "Human," or "Unrestricted Mode." You can also input a custom preset.

**Short-Term Memory** – The most recent messages from your chat history. You can control how much recent conversation history is preserved through the memory settings. There are also settings to completely disable short term memory on the AI Engine page.

**Long-Term Memory** – When enabled, your Shape can access and reference facts, events, and details from previous conversations. Long term memory is also managed on the AI Engine page and is very customizable.

**Knowledge & Training** – Custom training, lore, facts, and other knowledge entries you've provided to guide your Shape's responses and expertise.

All of this information is packaged together and sent to the AI engine with every message you send.

## How AI Engines Affect Context Windows

Different AI engines have varying **context window sizes**, measured in tokens (small chunks of text). A larger context window allows the AI to consider more information simultaneously—including more chat history, personality details, and knowledge entries. Conversely, a smaller context window means the AI may lose track of older conversation elements more quickly. If you need a larger context window, premium AI Engines such as GPT-5 offer the largest context windows available.

## What Happens When the Context Window Fills Up?

When the context window reaches capacity, the system must make room for new information by dropping older content. This can result in:

- The AI forgetting earlier parts of the conversation
- Loss of important details like names, events, or user preferences
- Responses that feel disconnected from the broader conversation context

## The Performance Impact of Information Overload

While it might seem beneficial to include as much information as possible, overwhelming the context window can actually hurt performance. Excessive amounts of knowledge entries, overly detailed instructions, or extremely long conversations can cause the AI to:

- Become confused and mix up details
- Generate shorter or lower-quality responses
- Hallucinate facts or ignore parts of its instructions

## Best Practices for Optimal Performance

To get the best results from your Shape:

**Keep it focused** – Write concise, targeted custom instructions and personality fields rather than lengthy descriptions.

**Be selective with knowledge** – Only include large amounts of knowledge when truly necessary for your Shape's purpose.

**Manage memory thoughtfully** – Consider whether you need every past message to remain in context, or if recent conversations are sufficient.

**Upgrade if necessary** – Shapes offers premium AI Engines with larger context windows. If your use case requires a large amount of input, you'll find the best results with premium.

## Key Takeaway

The context window serves as your Shape's working memory. Keeping it clear, focused, and well-organized helps ensure your Shape responds accurately, consistently, and with the right level of detail for meaningful conversations.
