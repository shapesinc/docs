Shapes has a live AI engine directory at [shapes.inc/engines](https://shapes.inc/engines). The list changes often, so use that page for the current engines.

This guide explains what the page means and how to pick an engine without needing to know model IDs.

## What You See on the Engines Page

At the top, you will see totals like:

- **AI Engines:** how many models are listed.
- **Providers:** how many model families or companies are available.
- **Always Free:** how many engines are free to use on Shapes.

Below that, you can search for an engine, provider, or capability. You can also filter by:

- **All:** shows every listed engine.
- **Free:** shows engines that do not use credits.
- **All families:** lets you filter by provider or model family.

Each engine card shows the provider, how new the engine is, the engine name, and badges like **Premium**, **Free on Shapes.inc**, **Reasoning**, **Native Vision**, **Tools**, **New**, or **Unstable**.

## What the Badges Mean

- **Free on Shapes.inc:** you can use it without Shape Credits.
- **Premium:** uses Shape Credits unless the chat has sponsored premium access.
- **Reasoning:** better for careful thinking, coding, planning, and hard questions.
- **Native Vision:** can understand images when the chat supports image input.
- **Tools:** can use supported Shapes tools and skills.
- **New:** recently added to the catalog.
- **Unstable:** may fail, change, or behave less reliably. Avoid using unstable engines as your main engine unless you are experimenting.

## Popular AI Model Families

These are general vibes, not strict rules. The best engine for a Shape is the one that feels right in chat.

### Claude AI Models

Models you may see: Claude Opus, Claude Sonnet, Claude Haiku, Claude Opus Latest, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and Claude Opus 4.7.

Anthropic Claude AI models are widely liked for writing, roleplay, emotional nuance, and staying in character.

- **Opus** is usually the strongest and most expensive Claude style.
- **Sonnet** is the balanced favorite for writing, roleplay, and smart conversation.
- **Haiku** is usually faster and lighter.

Pick Claude if you want a Shape that feels thoughtful, expressive, and good at long scenes.

### GPT AI Models

Models you may see: GPT-5.4, GPT-5 Nano, GPT-4o, GPT-4o Mini, GPT-3.5 Turbo, and other OpenAI GPT variants.

OpenAI GPT models are medium all-rounders in real chat use: not the most wild, not the weakest, usually predictable and steady. They are good at following instructions, coding, explaining things, and handling many different kinds of chats.

Compared with some other families, GPT models can feel a bit more filtered or restrained. That can be good for helpful assistant Shapes, but less exciting for loose roleplay or very expressive characters.

- **Full GPT models** are better for complex work and smart replies.
- **Mini / Nano** versions are faster and cheaper, and can be good for casual chat or fallback engines.

Pick GPT if you want a stable general-purpose Shape that can switch between chat, help, coding, and explanations.

### Gemini AI Models

Models you may see: Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite Preview, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Gemini 2.0 Flash, Gemini 2.0 Flash Lite, and other Gemini Flash or Pro variants.

Google Gemini AI models are often creative, flexible, and less restrained-feeling in chat. They are also strong at large context, image understanding, and mixed tasks. Flash and Lite versions are usually faster.

Pick Gemini if you want creative replies, speed, big-context chats, or image-friendly features.

### Gemma AI Models

Models you may see: Gemma 4 31B, Gemma 4 26B A4B, and other Gemma instruct models.

Gemma models are Google's more open model family. On Shapes, Gemma can be a good free choice for storytelling, freestyle chat, and general use.

Pick Gemma if you want a strong free option for casual chat, storytelling, or lightweight roleplay.

### DeepSeek AI Models

Models you may see: DeepSeek V4 Pro, DeepSeek V4 Flash, DeepSeek V3.2, DeepSeek V3, DeepSeek Chat, and DeepSeek R1-style models.

DeepSeek AI models can be surprisingly good at roleplay and are not usually seen as strongly filtered. Users also like them for reasoning, coding, and problem-solving.

The tradeoff is consistency. DeepSeek can feel weird: sometimes excellent, sometimes off-tone, and sometimes not as polished as the bigger premium families. Some newly added DeepSeek engines may appear as unstable, so check the badge before choosing one.

Pick DeepSeek if you want a Shape that can be smart, loose, and interesting, and you are okay with some uneven behavior.

### Grok AI Models

Models you may see: Grok 4.20, Grok 4.20 Multi-Agent, Grok 4, Grok Fast, and other xAI Grok variants.

xAI Grok models tend to feel expressive, direct, and not very restrained. They can be creative, punchy, and good for bold character voices or fast-moving conversations. Some Grok engines also support vision or reasoning depending on the badge.

Pick Grok if you want a Shape that feels bold, colorful, and conversational.

### Llama AI Models

Models you may see: Llama 3.3 70B Instruct, Llama 3.2 11B Vision Instruct, Llama 3.2 3B Instruct, Llama 3.1 70B Instruct, and Llama 3 8B Instruct.

Meta Llama AI models are usually low-cost, lower-reasoning engines compared with frontier premium models. They can still be good for freestyle chats, simple characters, entry-level roleplay, and fallback use.

Pick Llama if you want a familiar, flexible free engine and do not need the smartest possible reasoning.

### Qwen AI Models

Models you may see: Qwen3.6 Plus, Qwen2.5 72B Instruct, Qwen2.5 VL 72B Instruct, Qwen VL Plus, Qwen-Turbo, Qwen-Plus, and QwQ 32B.

Qwen AI models are often good at coding, multilingual use, structured answers, and long-context work. Qwen also has vision models when you see vision-related names or the **Native Vision** badge.

Pick Qwen if you want a practical engine for technical Shapes, multilingual Shapes, or organized replies.

### Mistral AI Models

Models you may see: Mistral Small Creative, Mistral Small 3, Mistral Nemo, Mistral Saba, and Mistral 7B Instruct.

Mistral AI models are usually fast and efficient. Some Mistral engines are tuned for creative writing or multilingual support.

Pick Mistral if you want a quick, lightweight engine that still feels capable.

### GLM AI Models

Models you may see: GLM 5.1, GLM 5V Turbo, and other Z.ai GLM variants.

Z.ai GLM models are often good at roleplay and can also handle reasoning or task-like conversations depending on the version.

Pick GLM if you want a Shape that can write well, roleplay well, and still stay useful for more complex chats.

### MiniMax AI Models

Models you may see: MiniMax M2-her, MiniMax M2.7, MiniMax-01, and other MiniMax chat models.

MiniMax AI models are often chosen for dialogue, storytelling, and character-heavy roleplay. They can be a good fit for Shapes that mostly live in casual scenes or fandom-style chats.

Pick MiniMax if your Shape is mostly for scenes, fandom chat, romance, adventure, or casual character interaction.

### Kimi AI Models

Models you may see: Kimi K2.6, Kimi K2.5, Kimi K2, and other MoonshotAI Kimi variants.

Moonshot Kimi AI models are usually very smart. They are strong for long context, general reasoning, and mixed creative or technical work.

Pick Kimi if you want a balanced engine for long chats, documents, or complicated prompts.

### Aion AI Models

Models you may see: Aion-2.0 and other AionLabs roleplay engines.

Aion AI models are roleplay-focused and can be strong for immersive, high-tension, character-driven scenes.

Pick Aion if your Shape is built for intense roleplay or long narrative conversations.

### TheDrummer, MythoMax, Rocinante, and Lunaris AI Models

Models you may see: TheDrummer models, MythoMax 13B, Rocinante 12B, UnslopNemo 12B, and Llama 3 8B Lunaris.

These AI model families are often used for creative writing, freestyle chat, and roleplay-heavy Shapes. They may not be the smartest engines, but they can feel natural for character interaction.

Pick these if you want a Shape that cares more about vibe and scene flow than strict reasoning.

### Other AI Model Families

Models you may see: MiMo, Trinity, Reka, Nova, Command R, Phi, Perplexity, NVIDIA, Baidu, Cohere, Amazon, Arcee, and Xiaomi engines.

You may see providers like Xiaomi, Arcee, Reka, Amazon, Cohere, Baidu, Perplexity, NVIDIA, and others. These can be great, but they are less familiar to many users.

If you are not sure, try them in a test chat before making them your Shape's main engine.

## Quick Picks

- **Best writing / roleplay:** try Anthropic Claude, GLM, MiniMax, Aion, or roleplay-focused engines.
- **Creative, less restrained chat:** try Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, or roleplay-focused engines.
- **Best all-around helper:** try Claude Sonnet, Kimi, Gemini, or GPT.
- **Best free daily chat:** try Gemma, Gemini Flash/Lite, GPT Nano/Mini, Llama, Mistral, or Qwen.
- **Best coding / reasoning:** try GPT, Claude Opus/Sonnet, DeepSeek, Qwen, Gemini Pro, or GLM.
- **Entry-level free RP:** try Llama, Gemma, Mistral, MiniMax, or TheDrummer-style engines.
- **Best image understanding:** look for **Native Vision**.
- **Best fallback:** choose a stable free engine with no **Unstable** badge.

## A Few Naming Hints

- **Pro / Opus / Plus:** usually stronger.
- **Sonnet:** strong balanced Claude model, especially popular for writing and RP.
- **Haiku / Mini / Nano / Lite / Flash:** usually faster and lighter.
- **Instruct:** built to follow instructions.
- **Vision / VL / V:** related to image understanding.
- **Preview / Latest:** can change more often.

You do not need to memorize these. If the engine sounds interesting, open its page, read the short description, and test it in chat.
